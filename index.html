// The Method

Three things every cycle is built on

No fad splits, no plan-of-the-month. Every client runs the same underlying framework — what changes is the load, the exercises, and the pace, based on what your body tells us each week.

PRINCIPLE

Push / Pull / Legs

Each muscle group gets trained twice a week with enough recovery between sessions to grow, not just get sore. It's the most field-tested split in strength training because it respects how the body actually recovers.

PRINCIPLE

Progressive overload

Every session is logged. When you hit your reps cleanly two sessions running, the weight moves up. Growth comes from a paper trail of small wins, not from chasing a pump.

PRINCIPLE

Coached, not just programmed

You get a real check-in every week — form review, load adjustments, and a plan for what changes next. The program reacts to you, not the other way around.

// The Program

Your 5-day Push, Pull, Legs split

This is the actual structure every new client starts from in week one. Tap a day to see the full session — exercises, sets, reps, and rest. Loads shown are starting points; your coach sets your real numbers after the first assessment.

Day 6 is optional conditioning or a weak-point accessory session (your call). Day 7 is full rest. Most clients repeat this 5-day week for a 4-week block before we re-test and adjust loads.

I don't believe in handing someone a PDF and calling it coaching.

Every client who walks in gets assessed first — mobility, current strength, old injuries, schedule. The Push/Pull/Legs split you see in the program section is the starting frame, but your sets, reps, and exercise choices come out of that assessment, not a template.

What stays consistent is the check-in. Every week, we look at what moved and what stalled, and the plan adjusts. That's the actual job — not writing a program once, but coaching the person running it.
